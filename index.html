Synthesizer 101 Guide

Oscillators & Octaves

The Oscillator is the sound source. Octave shifts the pitch by doubling or halving the frequency. Moving the slider to -1 drops the pitch by one full octave (deeper), while +1 makes it higher.

Fine Pitch: Use this to "detune" Osc 2 slightly from Osc 1 to create a thick, chorus-like sound.

The LFO (Modulator)

The Low Frequency Oscillator doesn't make sound you hear directly. Instead, it "wiggles" another setting.
• Target Filter: Creates a "wah-wah" effect.
• Target Pitch: Creates vibrato.
• Target Volume: Creates tremolo.

The Filter

Think of this as a tone sculptor.
• Cutoff: Sets the frequency where the filter starts working.
• Resonance: Boosts the volume at the cutoff point, making the sound "chirpy" or "squelchy".

ADSR (The Envelope)

Controls how the volume changes over time:
• Attack: How fast the sound starts (0 = instant, 1 = fade in).
• Decay: Time to drop to the Sustain level.
• Sustain: The volume held while the key is pressed.
• Release: How long the sound lingers after you let go.

Effects & Noise

Echo Mix: Adds a rhythmic delay to your notes.
Noise: Adds "texture" (hiss/static). White noise is bright, Brown noise is deep like a waterfall.

Controls

• Use your Mouse or Computer Keyboard (A, S, D, F...) to play.
• Press 'Z' or 'X' to shift the keyboard octave on the fly.
